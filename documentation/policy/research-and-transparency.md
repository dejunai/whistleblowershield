# Research and Transparency

This document describes how legal information is sourced, interpreted, and presented within the project.

The goal is to maintain accuracy, traceability, and clarity without overcomplicating the research process.

---

## Research Approach

Legal information is gathered and structured with an emphasis on:

- primary legal sources (statutes, regulations, official materials)  
- jurisdiction-specific accuracy  
- consistency across similar legal concepts  

Secondary sources may be used for context, but primary sources are preferred where available.

---

## Source Handling

When working with legal sources:

- identify the relevant jurisdiction  
- confirm the source is current and applicable  
- extract only what is necessary to support the concept being documented  

Avoid overloading content with unnecessary citations.

---

## Interpretation

Legal information is not presented as advice.

When interpreting sources:

- avoid making definitive legal conclusions  
- distinguish between what the law states and how it may be understood  
- acknowledge where interpretation may vary  

The goal is to clarify, not to decide outcomes.

---

## Traceability

Where practical, content should maintain a clear connection to underlying sources.

This may include:

- referencing statutes or regulations  
- linking concepts back to structured data  
- ensuring that guidance can be traced to supporting material  

Traceability does not need to be exhaustive, but it should be sufficient to support confidence in the information.

---

## Transparency

The project aims to be transparent about:

- how information is sourced  
- how it is interpreted  
- where limitations exist  

This does not require full disclosure of every decision, but should avoid presenting information as more certain or complete than it is.

---

## Limitations

Legal information may be:

- incomplete  
- subject to change  
- dependent on jurisdiction-specific context  

Content should avoid implying completeness where it cannot be guaranteed.

---

## Relationship to Other Systems

- The legal data system provides structured source material  
- Editorial content interprets and presents that material  
- Guidance connects it to user scenarios  

This document sits between those layers, supporting consistency in how information flows between them.

---

## Ongoing Refinement

This approach is iterative.

- Sources may be revisited  
- Interpretations may be adjusted  
- Transparency may be improved over time  

The goal is steady improvement, not complete coverage.
