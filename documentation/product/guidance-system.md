# Guidance System

This document describes how legal information is translated into user-facing guidance.

The goal is to help users understand their situation and possible next steps without oversimplifying the underlying law.

---

## Purpose

The guidance system exists to:

- connect structured legal information to real-world scenarios  
- provide clear, practical explanations  
- help users identify what may apply to their situation  
- maintain a link between guidance and underlying legal sources  

---

## Relationship to Other Systems

The guidance system sits on top of:

- the legal data system (structured legal information)  
- the editorial system (how content is written and maintained)  

It acts as the layer that connects those systems to the user.

---

## Scenario-Based Structure

Guidance is organized around user scenarios rather than legal categories.

Examples include:

- considering whether to report wrongdoing  
- experiencing retaliation after reporting  
- trying to understand legal protections  

This allows users to approach the system from their situation rather than needing prior legal knowledge.

---

## Layered Content Approach

Guidance typically follows a layered structure:

1. **Summary** — a clear, high-level explanation  
2. **Guidance** — practical interpretation or direction  
3. **Detail** — additional explanation or nuance  
4. **Sources** — connection to underlying legal material  

This structure is flexible, but helps balance clarity and depth.

---

## Decision-Oriented Content

Guidance should support decision-making without directing outcomes.

- highlight relevant considerations  
- outline possible paths or options  
- avoid prescribing specific actions  

The goal is to inform, not to instruct.

---

## Clarity and Limitations

Guidance should:

- avoid unnecessary legal jargon  
- acknowledge uncertainty where it exists  
- avoid implying guarantees or outcomes  

Legal situations can vary, and guidance should reflect that.

---

## Connection to Legal Data

Where practical, guidance should:

- reflect the structure of the underlying legal data  
- align with defined concepts and relationships  
- allow traceability back to source material  

Guidance does not need to expose all underlying structure, but should not contradict it.

---

## Use of Personas

Guidance is shaped by user personas.

- Persona A (pre-disclosure) influences exploratory and risk-focused content  
- Persona B (post-disclosure) influences response and protection-focused content  
- Persona C (research) influences completeness and structure  

Personas guide emphasis and framing, not rigid structure.

---

## Navigation

Guidance should allow users to:

- start from a scenario  
- move toward more detailed explanations  
- access supporting legal information when needed  

Navigation should feel natural, not forced through legal categories.

---

## Ongoing Refinement

The guidance system is iterative.

- content may be adjusted based on gaps or confusion  
- structure may evolve as new scenarios emerge  
- relationships to legal data may be refined over time  

The goal is to improve usability without losing accuracy.
