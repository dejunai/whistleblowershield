# Jurisdiction Scope Model

## Purpose

This document defines the jurisdiction hierarchy used by
WhistleblowerShield.

The platform focuses exclusively on United States law.

---

# Core Jurisdiction Levels

The U.S. legal system operates across multiple levels.

Whistleblower protections may exist at each level.

Hierarchy:

United States
 ├ Federal
 └ States
    ├ California
    ├ Texas
    ├ New York
    └ Other states

---

# Federal Jurisdiction

Federal laws apply across the entire United States.

Examples:

False Claims Act
Sarbanes-Oxley Act
Dodd-Frank Act

Federal agencies may also administer whistleblower programs.

Examples:

SEC
OSHA
IRS

---

# State Jurisdictions

States may implement their own whistleblower protections.

These laws typically apply only within the state.

Examples:

California whistleblower statutes
Texas whistleblower protections
New York labor protections

---

# Jurisdiction Relationships

Entities should reference the jurisdiction where the law applies.

Example relationships:

False Claims Act → United States
California Labor Code §1102.5 → California

---

# Multi-Jurisdiction Laws

Some laws may apply across multiple jurisdictions.

In these cases, the law should be attached to the primary
jurisdiction where it originates.

Example:

Federal statute → United States

---

# Future Expansion

Possible future jurisdiction categories:

territories
municipal laws
special regulatory jurisdictions

These should only be added if necessary.
