# U.S. Whistleblower Law Taxonomy

## Purpose

This document defines a classification system for
whistleblower laws in the United States.

The taxonomy helps organize statutes and legal
information in a consistent structure.

---

# Major Categories

Whistleblower laws generally fall into the
following categories.

---

# Fraud Against Government

These laws address fraud involving government funds.

Examples:

False Claims Act

Characteristics:

qui tam lawsuits  
financial awards  
federal enforcement

---

# Securities Law

These laws address fraud in financial markets.

Examples:

Dodd-Frank Act  
Sarbanes-Oxley Act

Characteristics:

SEC reporting systems  
confidential reporting  
financial incentives

---

# Workplace Retaliation

These laws protect employees who report misconduct.

Examples:

state whistleblower protection laws  
labor code protections

Characteristics:

anti-retaliation provisions  
employment remedies

---

# Tax Fraud

These laws address violations involving tax reporting.

Examples:

IRS whistleblower program

Characteristics:

financial awards  
confidential reporting

---

# Environmental Law

These laws protect individuals reporting
environmental violations.

Examples:

Clean Air Act whistleblower provisions  
Clean Water Act protections

Characteristics:

Department of Labor enforcement  
environmental compliance

---

# Public Sector Whistleblowing

Protections specific to government employees.

Examples:

Whistleblower Protection Act

Characteristics:

federal employee protections  
administrative complaint processes

---

# Future Categories

Additional categories may be introduced as needed.

Examples:

healthcare fraud  
procurement fraud  
banking regulation
