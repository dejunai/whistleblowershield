# Source Verification Policy

## Purpose

This document defines the standards used to verify legal sources
within the WhistleblowerShield platform.

The goal is to ensure that all legal information published by the
system is based on authoritative and verifiable sources.

---

# Source Reliability Tiers

Sources are ranked according to reliability.

Editors should prioritize higher tier sources whenever possible.

---

## Tier 1 â€” Primary Legal Sources

These are authoritative legal materials published by official
government bodies.

Examples include:

Federal statutes (United States Code)  
Federal regulations (Code of Federal Regulations)  
State statutes  
Court decisions  
Official agency publications  

Preferred websites:

govinfo.gov  
congress.gov  
federalregister.gov  
state legislature websites  
official court websites  

Tier 1 sources should be used whenever possible.

---

## Tier 2 â€” Government Guidance

Government agencies often publish interpretive guidance
related to whistleblower laws.

Examples:

SEC guidance documents  
Department of Labor guidance  
IRS program documentation  

These sources may explain how statutes are applied in practice.

---

## Tier 3 â€” Academic and Legal Analysis

Scholarly analysis can provide useful context.

Examples:

law review articles  
academic research publications  
legal textbooks  

These sources should support interpretation but not replace
primary legal authority.

---

## Tier 4 â€” Advocacy and Commentary

Advocacy organizations sometimes publish explanations
of whistleblower protections.

Examples:

nonprofit advocacy groups  
legal commentary articles  

These sources may provide helpful summaries but should
not be treated as authoritative.

---

# Prohibited Sources

Certain sources should not be used as primary references.

Examples include:

blogs without legal citations  
anonymous commentary  
marketing or promotional material  
unverified online summaries  

These sources may contain inaccuracies.

---

# Citation Requirements

Whenever legal claims are made, they should reference
at least one authoritative source.

Preferred citations include:

statutes  
regulations  
court decisions  
official agency publications  

---

# Editorial Verification

Editors should verify:

that citations are accurate  
that links point to official sources  
that summaries reflect the underlying law  

When uncertainty exists, the underlying statute or regulation
should be reviewed directly.

---

# Future Automation

Future versions of the ws-core plugin may include:

source validation tools  
citation integrity checks  
automated link verification

These tools will help maintain data quality as the
dataset grows.
