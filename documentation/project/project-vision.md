# Project Vision

## What This Platform Is

WhistleblowerShield is a free, nonpartisan, public-interest
reference for U.S. whistleblower protection laws — covering
all 50 states, the District of Columbia, U.S. territories,
and federal law.

It exists for one reason: ordinary people facing extraordinary
situations need accurate, plain-language information about
their legal rights — and that information is currently scattered,
technical, and hard to find.

---

## The Problem We Are Solving

When a worker witnesses fraud, misconduct, or illegal activity
at their employer, they face an immediate and urgent question:
if I report this, am I protected?

The answer exists in law. But legal information is typically:

- Written for attorneys, not workers
- Scattered across government websites, agency pages,
  and legal databases
- Inconsistent in accuracy and currency
- Absent entirely for many states and territories

WhistleblowerShield exists to bridge that gap — providing
structured, sourced, and accessible legal information to
people who need it most, at the moment they need it.

---

## The Two-Layer Architecture

The platform is built on two complementary layers that serve
different purposes but depend on each other completely.

### Public Guidance Layer

The public site is built for workers, not lawyers.

It presents whistleblower protection law in plain English,
organized around the questions people actually ask:

- Am I protected if I report this?
- What can my employer legally do to me?
- Who do I report to, and how long do I have?
- What help is available if I'm already facing retaliation?

Clarity is not a compromise here. It is the goal.

### Legal Knowledge Layer

Underneath the public guidance is a structured legal archive.

The archive organizes legal information about each jurisdiction
into discrete, structured records: summaries, statutes,
procedures, resources, and legal updates. Every factual claim
is sourced to a primary legal authority. Every page displays
authorship, review status, and the date content was last verified.

The archive exists to ensure the guidance layer is accurate,
verifiable, and maintainable as law changes over time.

**The archive serves the guidance. The guidance serves the user.**

---

## Who We Are Building This For

Three people drive every design and editorial decision on this
platform. They are defined in detail in user-personas.md.

**Maya** has just witnessed misconduct at work. She is scared,
unsure whether she qualifies as a whistleblower, and doesn't
know whether coming forward will cost her her job. She needs
a plain-language answer to whether the law is on her side —
fast, and without feeling like she's walked into a law firm.

**James** has already come forward and is now facing retaliation.
He was demoted last week. He needs to know what agency to contact,
what the filing deadline is, and whether what is happening to him
is illegal. He needs that information today.

**Daniel** is a journalist or legal researcher verifying the scope
of a federal statute for an article he's writing. He needs accurate
citations, primary source links, and enough editorial transparency
to cite the platform with confidence.

All three matter. Maya and James are why the platform exists.
Daniel represents the standard of rigor it must meet.

---

## Editorial Commitments

The platform holds itself to two standards simultaneously:
legal accuracy and genuine usefulness to someone without
legal training. Both must be met. Neither excuses the other.

Every jurisdiction page displays:

- A "Last Reviewed" date reflecting the most recent substantive
  review of the content
- Human review and legal review status badges, set only when
  review has genuinely occurred
- Full source citations linked to primary official sources
- Author attribution

These are not decorative features. They are the mechanism
by which users can evaluate whether to trust what they read.

The platform is honest about its limitations. It is maintained
by an amateur legal researcher, not a law firm. It acknowledges
when content is pending review. It corrects errors transparently
and documents changes in a public legal updates log.

---

## Nonpartisan Stance

WhistleblowerShield has no political affiliation and accepts
no donor or commercial influence over editorial content.

Whistleblower protections are a matter of law, not politics.
The platform describes what the law does — who it covers,
what it prohibits, what remedies it provides — without
characterizing outcomes as political victories or defeats,
and without advocating for or against any policy position.

---

## Long-Term Ambition

If this platform succeeds, it becomes the most accessible and
reliable structured reference for U.S. whistleblower protection
law — a resource that workers, journalists, researchers, and
advocates can rely on and cite with confidence.

It will never be the only resource someone needs. But it can
be the resource that helps them understand their rights clearly
enough to take the next step — whether that is finding an
attorney, filing a complaint, or simply knowing that the law
sees what happened to them.

That is the standard this platform is built to meet.
