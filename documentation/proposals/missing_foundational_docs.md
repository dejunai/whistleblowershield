# Proposal: Missing Foundational Documentation

Status: Draft  
Purpose: Identify documentation commonly missing in early public-interest platforms that becomes important as projects grow.

This proposal highlights documentation that may become necessary if WhistleblowerShield attracts collaborators, journalists, or donors.

The goal is not to create unnecessary bureaucracy, but to ensure the project can scale responsibly while maintaining its core philosophy.

---

## Project Context

WhistleblowerShield is currently developed by a single maintainer with limited resources.

The project prioritizes:

- plain-language guidance for whistleblowers
- accurate legal information
- transparent editorial practices

Early development focuses on building the foundation for a reliable and trustworthy platform.

Because the project may grow in the future, documenting certain principles early can prevent confusion later.

---

## 1. Project Status and Development Model

Recommended file:

/documentation/project/project_status.md

Purpose:

Explain the current development reality of the project.

Topics may include:

- solo development
- limited infrastructure
- live-site prototyping
- documentation-first design approach

Why this matters:

External contributors and donors often want to understand how mature a project is and what constraints currently exist.

Being transparent builds trust and manages expectations.

---

## 2. Data Integrity and Update Policy

Recommended file:

/documentation/editorial/data_update_policy.md

Purpose:

Describe how legal information is kept accurate over time.

Possible topics:

- how legal updates are tracked
- how statutes are reviewed
- how outdated content is identified
- update frequency expectations

Why this matters:

Legal information platforms must address the problem of **law changing over time**.

Even a simple update policy helps establish credibility.

---

## 3. Scope and Coverage Policy

Recommended file:

/documentation/project/scope_and_coverage.md

Purpose:

Define what the platform intends to cover and what it does not.

Possible topics:

- jurisdictions currently supported
- areas of law included
- areas intentionally excluded
- criteria for adding new jurisdictions

Why this matters:

Without a scope policy, projects can slowly expand in inconsistent ways.

Defining scope early prevents mission drift.

---

## 4. Risk and Liability Awareness

Recommended file:

/documentation/project/legal_risk_awareness.md

Purpose:

Explain how the platform distinguishes between:

- legal information
- legal advice

Possible topics:

- informational nature of content
- encouragement to seek professional counsel
- responsible framing of legal explanations

Why this matters:

Public-interest legal resources must carefully communicate their role as informational platforms.

Documenting this internally ensures consistent messaging.

---

## 5. Contributor Onboarding Overview

Recommended file:

/documentation/project/contributing_overview.md

Purpose:

Provide future collaborators with a simple explanation of how the project works.

Topics may include:

- project philosophy
- documentation structure
- editorial standards
- how research contributions should be prepared

Why this matters:

If the project eventually attracts volunteers or collaborators, clear onboarding documentation can prevent confusion and maintain quality.

---

## Guiding Principle

These documents should remain lightweight and practical.

The goal is not to introduce unnecessary complexity, but to ensure that as the project grows it can maintain:

- clarity
- trust
- consistency
- alignment with the core mission

---

## Core Mission Reminder

WhistleblowerShield exists primarily to provide **clear, plain-language guidance for workers who need to understand whistleblower protections**.

The legal database and documentation structure exist to support that mission.

All future documentation should reinforce this guiding principle.

---

End of proposal.
