# Editorial System

This document describes how content is written, structured, and maintained within the project.

The goal is to produce content that is clear, accurate, and consistent, while remaining practical and usable.

---

## Core Approach

Content should:

- prioritize clarity without oversimplifying legal meaning  
- remain factually accurate and grounded in source material  
- avoid unnecessary complexity or jargon  
- stay focused on the user’s situation and questions  

Perfect consistency is not required, but avoid introducing confusion.

---

## Relationship to the System

Editorial work operates within a structured system:

- Legal information is defined and organized in the data layer  
- Editorial content interprets and presents that information  
- Guidance connects content to real-world user scenarios  

Editorial decisions should align with the structure of the underlying system, but do not need to mirror it exactly.

---

## Writing Style

### Clarity

- Use plain, direct language  
- Avoid unnecessary legal jargon where possible  
- When technical terms are needed, provide enough context for understanding  

---

### Accuracy

- Do not overstate legal conclusions  
- Avoid implying certainty where none exists  
- Ensure statements can be supported by underlying legal sources  

---

### Practical Focus

- Emphasize what the user is likely trying to understand or decide  
- Avoid abstract explanations unless they support a practical point  
- Keep content grounded in real-world scenarios  

---

## Content Structure

Content should generally follow a layered approach:

1. **Summary** — a clear, high-level explanation  
2. **Guidance** — practical interpretation or next steps  
3. **Detail** — supporting explanation or nuance  
4. **Sources** — connection to underlying legal material  

Not all content needs to strictly follow this structure, but it should be used as a general guide.

---

## Workflow

The editorial workflow is iterative rather than linear.

Typical flow:

1. Identify or update a legal concept or dataset  
2. Draft or revise related content  
3. Align content with the underlying data structure  
4. Review for clarity and accuracy  
5. Refine as needed over time  

Work does not need to proceed in strict order. Content may be revisited and adjusted as the system evolves.

---

## Consistency

Consistency is preferred, but not strictly enforced.

- Similar concepts should be described in similar ways where practical  
- Avoid introducing conflicting descriptions of the same idea  
- Minor variation in phrasing is acceptable  

When in doubt, prioritize clarity over strict uniformity.

---

## Use of Personas

Personas represent end-users of the system, not the audience of this documentation.

They should be considered when:

- framing explanations  
- deciding what information to emphasize  
- structuring guidance  

Do not restate full persona definitions here. Refer to the root README if needed.

---

## Ongoing Refinement

This system is not fixed.

- Content may be revised as understanding improves  
- Structure may evolve over time  
- Inconsistencies may be corrected gradually  

The goal is steady improvement, not perfect alignment.
