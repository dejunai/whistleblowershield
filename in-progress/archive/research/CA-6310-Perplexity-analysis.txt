## Proposals — still zero across all three

CA-6310 had at least one genuine proposal opportunity: the family-member protection extension, which all three models described in `adverseactionscope` or `reviewnotes` but none proposed as a taxonomy term. That concept — retaliation against an employee *because of* a family member's protected activity — appears in multiple California statutes and almost certainly appears in other jurisdictions. It is a textbook proposal candidate.

The revised proposal language got closer — ChatGPT and Grok both surfaced the concept in notes — but neither crossed the threshold into `newtermsproposed`. This suggests the proposal pathway needs one more nudge: a concrete example of what a good proposal looks like, not just the shape of the proposal object.

1. **Add law firm websites to the forbidden citation sources list** — explicitly name them alongside `scholar.google.com`. Grok's `gmsr.com` slip shows the approved-source list needs a negative example for non-institutional non-aggregator sites.

3. **Consider dropping Gemini Flash from the rotation entirely** — two consecutive runs with `limitambiguous: false` on derived SOLs, two consecutive forbidden citation sources, and two consecutive useless integrity blocks is a pattern. If you want to keep Gemini in rotation, test Gemini Pro or Advanced on the next statute before committing.
