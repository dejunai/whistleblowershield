## What needs attention

### Citations — zero across all five records
This is the most significant gap. Five California statutes, zero citations, all omitted with integrity flags citing inability to verify stable URLs. That is honest behavior, but for statutes like `CA-8547` and `CA-12653` with real appellate history, it is a quality gap. The integrity entries reference *Miklosy* for `CA-8547` — that is a real California Supreme Court case on exhaustion — but Pro chose omission over risk rather than attempting an approved-source lookup. ChatGPT and Grok have both produced verified citations from `law.justia.com` and `courtlistener.com` in prior runs. Pro may need explicit encouragement to attempt approved secondary sources before giving up on citations entirely.

### `CA-8547 limitambiguous: false` with 12 months — verify
The 12-month deadline for `CA-8547` does appear in Government Code 8547.12, but it is worth confirming whether that deadline is in the section itself or a related section, since the `statuteid` is `CA-8547` (the parent section) not `CA-8547.12`. If the deadline is in a subsection rather than the entry-point section, `limitambiguous` should arguably be `true`. Flag for human review.

### `CA-12653 limitambiguous: false` with 3 years — verify
The False Claims Act retaliation SOL under Government Code 12653 is worth confirming. The federal FCA has a 3-year/6-year dual SOL; California's version may differ. Pro's confidence here deserves a reviewer check before approval.

### `burdenofproofflag: true` on two records
Same issue as Grok in the prior run — `burdenofproofflag` is populated with `true` rather than a short signal phrase. The schema says to omit it unless a meaningful burden shift is identified, and to use a signal phrase not a boolean. Worth checking whether your schema note addition from the prior run made it into v2.0.7.1 — it does not appear to be in the attached prompt.

### `CA-8547 disclosuretargets: [external-state]` only
This is thin. Government Code 8547 protects disclosures to the State Auditor, the State Personnel Board, the Joint Legislative Audit Committee, and supervisors. `internal-management` and `legislature` should both be present. This is under-tagging rather than over-tagging, which is the safer error, but worth enrichment.

### `CA-232.5 disclosuretargets` includes `public` and `media`
This is the most aggressive disclosure target tagging in the batch. Lab Code 232.5 protects employees who disclose information about working conditions — but whether `media` and `public` are explicitly named as targets in the statute text, or inferred from the nature of the disclosure, is worth a reviewer check. The prompt instructs "tag all valid reporting targets explicitly named or clearly implied" — `media` and `public` feel like inferences here rather than explicit statutory language.

### `jsonrunnotes: "Clinical execution"` — again
Pro used the same self-congratulatory phrase as Flash. It is less egregious here because the rest of the output is genuinely better, but it is still not a useful note. The only actionable content in `jsonrunnotes` is the list of `has-details` usages. The rest is performative. Worth adding a prompt note: "`jsonrunnotes` should describe decisions, gaps, and uncertainties — not evaluate the quality of your own output."

### Zero proposals — still
Five records, at least two real proposal candidates:
- **`family-member-of-whistleblower`** — the family-member example is literally in the prompt, and `CA-1102.5` is in the exclude list, so Pro knows it was seen before. A model reading carefully should have proposed it consolidated across the two statutes it was seen in.
- **`medical-staff-privileges`** as an adverse action type — the `CA-1278.5` `adverseactiondetails` describes "restriction, revocation, or unfavorable changes in medical staff privileges" which is a real and recurring concept in healthcare whistleblower law that has no slug. Pro used `has-details` correctly but did not propose the term.

## Strategic read

**Gemini Pro is now your batch model.** When you need 5-record runs across a jurisdiction, Pro's statute selection instincts are solid and its schema discipline has caught up. Flash is still retired.

**The citation gap is Pro's biggest remaining weakness.** It is choosing omission too aggressively on citations. A targeted note — "before omitting a citation, attempt at least one approved secondary source lookup; omit only after a genuine attempt fails" — may be enough to unlock citations in the next run without pushing it toward fabrication.

**The proposal gap is now your most persistent cross-model problem.** Three models, multiple runs, only one proposal ever produced (Grok on SOX). The family-member example is in the prompt and still not being generalized. Consider adding a second worked example on a different concept — `medical-staff-privileges` from this run would be a perfect candidate, since it appeared in this batch and is unambiguously a real gap.

**Three prompt actions from this run:**

1. **Citation attempt instruction** — add to CITATIONS section: "Before omitting a citation, make at least one genuine attempt at an approved secondary source. Omit only after that attempt fails. Omitting without attempting is a quality gap worth flagging in integrity."

2. **`jsonrunnotes` self-evaluation prohibition** — add to jsonrunnotes section: "Do not evaluate the quality of your own output in this field. Describe decisions, gaps, and uncertainties only."

3. **Second proposal example** — add `medical-staff-privileges` or a similar concept as a second worked example in the PROPOSING section to show that single-jurisdiction concepts with no existing slug are also worth proposing, not just cross-jurisdiction patterns.
