## Strategic read

**ChatGPT with the Thinking layer** is now your most trustworthy single-model output. The planning pass before execution is visibly improving `jsonrunnotes` quality and SOL discipline simultaneously. The tradeoff is conservative taxonomy depth — it under-tags where Grok correctly tags.

**Grok** is your richest output but has one new failure mode to address: the `public-body` invented slug. One prompt addition fixes that. If you close that gap, Grok becomes the strongest single-model producer.

**Gemini Flash** is the weakest of the three for this use case. Speed is its only advantage here, and for legal data accuracy you do not want the speed-optimized model. The `limitambiguous: false` error on a derivied SOL, combined with the forbidden Google Scholar URL, means Gemini Flash outputs need heavier human review than the other two. Worth testing Gemini Pro or Gemini Advanced if you want to keep Gemini in the rotation.

## One prompt addition to make now

Add this line to the TAXONOMY FIELDS section:

```
Do not insert any slug that does not appear verbatim in the
taxonomy tables provided. If no listed slug fits, leave the
array empty and note the gap in jsonrunnotes. Do not invent
or approximate a slug.
```

That closes the `public-body` failure mode for Grok without adding significant prompt length.
