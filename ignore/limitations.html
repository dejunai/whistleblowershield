Limitations and Ramifications

Unprotected Acts: Protections generally do not extend to the disclosure of personal grievances or disagreements regarding lawful policy decisions that do not involve a violation of law or regulation.

Media Reporting: Direct reporting to news media is generally not a protected disclosure under California’s primary whistleblower statutes. To secure legal protection, report to a government agency or person with authority first.

Malicious Claims: California law does not protect disclosures that are known to be false or made with reckless disregard for the truth (including reports made with the intent to cause harm rather than expose wrongdoing). Employers may have grounds for legal recourse if a report is proven to be intentionally malicious.
