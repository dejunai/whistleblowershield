Get Help: Whistleblower Resources

Navigating the whistleblower process can be complex. This directory provides a curated list of government reporting channels, nonprofit advocacy groups, and legal referral services to help you determine your next steps. For example: if you are a federal employee facing retaliation, the Office of Special Counsel (OSC) is your primary channel; if you need legal, communications, or security support, Whistleblower Aid offers assistance to whistleblowers.


1. National Advocacy & Legal Nonprofits

These organizations provide educational materials, advocacy, and in some cases, case evaluation for whistleblowers across various sectors.

  • National Whistleblower Center (NWC): A leading nonprofit focused on environmental, tax, and securities fraud advocacy. Visit Website
  • Government Accountability Project (GAP): Provides legal support and advocacy for employees exposing Official Misconduct. Visit Website
  • Whistleblower Aid: A nonprofit law firm providing Pro Bono legal, communications, and security support for whistleblowers reporting lawbreaking. Visit Website
  • Whistleblowers of America (WoA): Specializes in peer support and assistance for whistleblowers suffering from workplace retaliation and trauma. Visit Website

2. Federal Reporting Channels

Official government bodies established to receive and investigate disclosures of wrongdoing at the federal level.

  • U.S. Office of Special Counsel (OSC): The primary agency for federal employees to report misconduct and Hatch Act violations. Visit Website
  • SEC Office of the Whistleblower: For reporting violations of federal Securities Laws, including corporate fraud or insider trading. Visit Website
  • OSHA Whistleblower Protection Program: Manages protections for employees in the workplace across more than 20 federal statutes. Visit Website

3. Legal Referral Services

If you need to find a private attorney who specializes in whistleblower or employment law, these referral services are a neutral starting point.

  • American Bar Association (ABA) Referral: Connects users to accredited state and local bar association referral services. Visit Website

Disclaimer: WhistleblowerShield.org is an educational initiative and does not provide legal representation. Inclusion in this directory does not constitute an endorsement. We strongly recommend consulting with a qualified attorney before initiating a formal disclosure.
