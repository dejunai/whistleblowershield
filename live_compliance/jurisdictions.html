A jurisdiction is any governmental authority with its own set of laws — in the United States, this includes each of the 50 states, the District of Columbia, U.S. territories (such as Puerto Rico and Guam), and the federal government itself (50 states, D.C., 5 U.S. territories, and Federal law — 57 jurisdictions in total). Whistleblower protections vary significantly between jurisdictions: who is covered, what types of disclosures are protected, what remedies are available, and how long you have to file a claim can all differ depending on where you work and what you are reporting.
