SECTION 2 — TAXONOMY RULES (REVISED v1.6.5)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

2.1 Absolute Controlled Vocabulary
All taxonomy values MUST match the predefined slugs in Section 4 exactly (kebab-case, lowercase). Do not modify, pluralize, or abbreviate. If a term is not in Section 4, it MUST NOT enter the record; it must be moved to new_terms_proposed.

2.2 Combinatorial Classification (Anti-Fracture Rule)
Before proposing a new term, determine if the legal concept can be accurately represented by combining existing terms.

Example: A statute protecting "Healthcare Whistleblowers reporting Fraud" should use healthcare-staff + fraud-false-claims. Do not propose healthcare-fraud-whistleblower.

Propose a new term ONLY if the concept is a distinct legal "species" that cannot be described by existing "traits."

2.3 Multi-Tagging Requirement
Assign all applicable tags. A statute is rarely a single-issue law. Include the Subject Matter (e.g., occupational-health-safety), the Protection Type (e.g., retaliation-protection), and the specific Procedure (e.g., qui-tam).

2.4 Precision vs. Approximation
"Close enough" is not acceptable for legal data. If an existing term is so broad it loses legal meaning (e.g., using corporate-staff for a police-officer), a new term MUST be proposed.

2.5 Term Proposal Triggers
Propose a term in the meta header if:

A recurring, legally meaningful class is missing (e.g., veterinary-staff or environmental-protection).

The existing vocabulary requires "tag-stuffing" more than 4 tags to describe one simple concept.

A specific statutory definition creates a unique class with different SOL or Remedy rules.

2.6 False Precision & Redundancy
Do NOT propose terms for:

Synonyms of existing terms.

Sub-sets of a category already covered by a different taxonomy (e.g., don't propose fired-whistleblower because termination exists in ws_remedies).

One-off examples that are unlikely to recur in other jurisdictions.

2.7 Proposal Integrity
Every entry in new_terms_proposed must:

Include a term_label (Human readable) and term_id (kebab-case).

Provide a notes justification explaining why existing tags could not be combined to represent the concept.

List all statutes in the current batch where this term was "seen_in".
